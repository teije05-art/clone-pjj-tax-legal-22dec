We are pleased to share with you the recent changes on compulsory Social Insurance (“SI”), Health Insurance (“HI”) and Unemployment Insurance (“UI”) contribution policies for your information and further action.
Regulatory changes 
Recently, the below two new laws and its guidance have been issued, taking effective from 01 January 2015: 
Law amending the Law on Health Insurance No. 46/2014/QH13 dated 13 June 2014 and Decree 105/2014/ND-CP dated 15 November 2014 guiding on the implementation of certain article or the Law on Health Insurance; and
Law on Employment No. 38/2013/QH13 dated 16 November 2013 (“Law No. 38”).
The Law on Social Insurance No. 71/2006/QH11 dated 29 June 2006 and its guiding regulations are still effective until 01 January 2016. 
Notable points 
In the meantime of waiting for guidance on the implementation of the Law No. 38, some provincial Department of Labor, Invalid and Social Affairs (“DOLISA”) have released the initial guidance based on the legislation of the Law with notable points as below:
Broaden the applicable objects of compulsory SI, HI, UI 
Including those who work under labor contracts with term for full three (03) months or more, disregard of how many total number of employees working for such employers (previously, only organizations having from ten (10) staff or more are subject to UI contribution).
Salary base for SI, HI, UI contribution
For those who are remunerated based on the salary rate stipulated by the State, the salary calculated by the salary rate and the basis salary shall be used, capped at 20 times of the basic salary.
For those who are remunerated as per the regulations of the employers, salary base for SI, HI, UI contribution is the contractual salary with minimum and maximum caps are as below:
For SI: the minimum is capped at the regional minimum salary and the maximum is capped at 20 times of the basic salary;
For HI: the maximum is capped at 20 times of the basic salary; 
For UI: the minimum is capped at the regional minimum salary and the maximum is capped at 20 times of the regional minimum salary, meaning that the salary base for UI contribution shall vary depending on the area from which the employers are located.
